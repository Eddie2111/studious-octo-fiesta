Bridging Worlds: Enhancing Sign Language Recognition and Seamless BdSL-to-Bengali Conversion with Sequential LSTM Innovation

"Bridging Worlds: Enhancing Sign Language Recognition and Seamless BdSL-to-Bengali Conversion with Sequential LSTM Innovation" is a project that integrates cutting-edge technologies to improve sign language recognition and facilitate the smooth conversion between Bangladesh Sign Language (BdSL) and Bengali. The project employs MediaPipe for feature extraction, capturing intricate details from sign language videos. A web application is utilized to collect textual data associated with the signs, while a sign language instructor contributes by providing expert guidance and meaningful annotations through video demonstrations.
The core innovation lies in the implementation of Sequential Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks, a type of recurrent neural network (RNN), for processing and predicting accurate data. The sequential LSTM model enhances the system's ability to understand and interpret the sequential nature of sign language, enabling more precise recognition and conversion. By combining these technologies, the project aims to bridge the communication gap between the Deaf and hearing communities by providing an efficient and accurate means of translating BdSL to Bengali, promoting inclusivity and accessibility in communication.


Securing the Pharmaceutical Supplychain Using Blockchain

"Securing the Pharmaceutical Supply Chain Using Blockchain" is an innovative solution leveraging cutting-edge technologies to enhance the security and transparency of the pharmaceutical supply chain. This project employs Next.js, a modern and efficient React framework, to develop a robust web application that facilitates seamless interactions and data flow within the supply chain ecosystem.
Central to this initiative is the implementation of blockchain technology, specifically utilizing AWS-managed Ethereum, to establish an immutable and decentralized ledger. This blockchain solution ensures the integrity of data across the entire supply chain, offering a secure and transparent record of transactions from manufacturers through distributors to end consumers.
Furthermore, the system utilizes an online-managed PostgreSQL database to store and manage critical information related to pharmaceutical products, enhancing data reliability and accessibility. By combining Next.js, online-managed databases, and AWS-managed Ethereum, the project not only secures the pharmaceutical supply chain against potential threats such as counterfeiting or unauthorized tampering but also provides stakeholders with real-time visibility into the movement and authenticity of pharmaceutical products. This initiative marks a significant step forward in fostering trust, accountability, and safety within the pharmaceutical industry.
